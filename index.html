Alfredo James "Al" Pacino

Oscar-winning actor Al Pacino has kept moviegoers riveted since the 1970s, with roles in films like 'The Godfather,' 'Dog Day Afternoon,' 'Serpico,' 'Dick Tracy' and 'Scent of a Woman.'. He is an American actor of stage and screen, filmmaker, and screenwriter.

Remarkable Characters

1. Michael Corleone (The Godfather trilogy)
Michael Corleone is the main protagonist of Mario Puzo's novel The Godfather and the three films based upon it, all directed by Francis Ford Coppola. He was portrayed in the film trilogy by Al Pacino, who was twice nominated for Academy Awards for the part.
2. Sonny Wortzik (Dog Day Afternoon)
Sonny Wortzik is the leading role which was inspired by P. F. Kluge's article "The Boys in the Bank" which was based on robbery of a Brooklyn bank by John Wojtowicz and Salvatore Naturale on August 22, 1972. John Wojtowicz character was portrayed by Al Pacino, who was nominated as lead actor for Academy Award and Golden Globe for his part.
3. Tony Montana (Scarface)
Antonio "Tony" Montana is a fictional character and the main protagonist of the 1983 film Scarface. He was portrayed by Al Pacino in the movie. Tony Montana has become a cultural icon and is one of the most famous movie characters of all time. In 2008, Montana was named the 27th Greatest Movie Character by Empire Magazine.
4. Frank Serpico (Serpico)
Frank Serpico based on the true story of New York City policeman Frank Serpico, who went undercover to expose the corruption of fellow officers.He was portrayed by Al Pacino in the movie and won Golden Globe for lead actor and nominated for Academy Award.

Accolades

He won Academy Award as Best Leading Actor for "Scent of a Woman"
He won Golden Globe Award as Best Leading Actor for "Serpico"
He won BAFTA Film Award as Best Leading Actor for "Dog Day Afternoon and The Godfather Part II"
the American Film Institute awarded Pacino with a lifetime achievement award
